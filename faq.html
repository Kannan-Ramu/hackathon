Frequenty Asked Questions

  • How does farm household income compare with the income of other US households?

    Since the mid-1990s, farm households have consistently out-earned non-farm households. This is due to both farm operation income as well as remunerative off-farm employment.

  • What is crop insurance?

    Because agriculture is inherently unpredictable, it is important to take preventative measures to mitigate some of the damage farmers face when crops fail. That’s where crop insurance comes in. Crop insurance helps to stabilize farmers’ incomes by protecting them against losses, minimizing debts and protecting crop yields.

  • How many farms are there in the world?

    There are more than 570 million farms worldwide, with over 90% of them being run by an individual or a family. Family farms produce around 80% of the world’s food.

  • What crops can be grown indoors?

    The great thing about indoor farming is that seasonal crops can be grown during any time and in any climate. Crops like strawberries, peppers, herbs, leafy greens, microgreens, and more can be grown indoors.

  • What is vertical farming?

    Vertical farming is the practice and process of growing crops and produce within a vertically stacked layer. As a type of indoor farming, vertical farming uses CEA or controlled environment agriculture, which helps to optimize plant growth by using different techniques like hydroponics, aquaponics, and aeroponics.

  • What is a container farm?

    Container farms allow farmers to control the growing environment of their plants, such as temperature and humidity. This also means that farmers don’t have to worry about harmful weather patterns, incompatible climates, or seasons ending.
